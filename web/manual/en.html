How to play it?

The screen shows the playing field. The left and bottom are the user interaction areas. The right side is the enemy's area.

  1. The area where the cards used by you or your opponent will be stored.
  2. The area of ​​your fort, the number 20 is the value of the fort at the moment. The default is 20, you can change in the application settings. Also, by default, when you reach level 100 of your fort, you will win.
  3. The level of your shaft, number 15 is the current shaft value, it is also the default shaft value at the start of the game, his can be changed in the application settings.
  4. Accordingly, the current values ​​of the enemy's fort.
  5. Accordingly, the current values ​​of the enemy shaft.
  1. An area indicating who is currently walking.
  2. User avatar (left) and opponent (right), when pressed, a menu with applied correctors is shown resources.
  3. Area of ​​Green resources of the user (left) and the opponent (right). Resources for applying green group cards (Money), cards in mainly aimed at building your fort and causing damage to the green group of enemy resources, slowing down the construction of the enemy's fort.
  4. Area of ​​Red-brown resources of the user (left) and the opponent (right). Resources for applying maps red-brown group (Technology), cards in mainly aimed at building your shaft and causing damage to the red-brown group of enemy resources, slowing down the construction of the opponent's shaft.
  5. Area of ​​Blue resources of the user (left) and the opponent (right). Resources for applying blue group cards (Units), cards in mainly aimed at attacking the opponent's fort and shaft, as well as causing damage and slowing down the receipt of resources of the blue group by the enemy.

Shows how much each resource will be added in one move to you or your opponent.

Shows how many resources you or your opponent have on the given move.

Shows how many additional resources of any of the groups will be added to you or your opponent per turn.

At the bottom is the player's card block. At the bottom of the map on the left and right in the corner is the cost of the map in resources, color indicates what kind of resource is required Maps of group 1 are darkened and they cannot be walked because there is not enough for their resources, they can only be pulled down and dropped. The cards group 2 are bright and can be walked by pulling them up.
